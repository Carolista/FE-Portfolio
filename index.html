A Lifelong Love Affair with Computers

My journey into tech has been almost entirely non-traditional, but a long time coming. From my earliest days learning DOS on a Tandy, to a couple of required CS classes in college, then 20 years in the nonprofit sector working with whatever cobbled-together technology I had available to me, I have generally been the person that everyone comes to for help with their computers, whether it was my job or not. (It wasn't, but I am always happy to help!)

It took a battle with cancer in 2017 to wake me up to the realization that I wanted to do whatever it took to switch to a career in tech. Enter LaunchCode — and the many wonderful people who taught me, mentored me, and encouraged me along the way — and I have achieved my dream! As a web developer, software engineer, and mentor to students, I love that I am constantly learning new things, and I am so looking forward to continuing this journey ahead of me.

Timeline

1983 My dad brought home a TRS-80 from Radio Shack, and I picked up DOS quickly so I could play on it. My favorite thing was making pictures out of characters, line by line, to be printed out on the dot matrix printer. In a way, I think this was my first introduction to coding concepts.
1985 I have good memories of playing text adventure games like Inca Temple and having a "conversation" with ELIZA on the Tandy.
1986 I got the coveted NES for Christmas, and was in love. I still have my console hooked up to my TV, and from time to time I pull out Super Mario Bros., the Legend of Zelda, Tetris, Ultima, or others for a good dose of nostalgia. I think it was during this period that I really became fascinated with the idea about how these programs were created even though I didn't know what code looked like yet.
1991 When she wasn't using it for her college homework, I used my mom's Commodore Colt to play the original (monochrome!) SimCity and to practice my typing skills.
1995-1997 In college I was given my first e-mail address and also my own web page as part of an Intro to CS class in the engineering school. I learned HTML (such as it was), checked my e-mail in PINE using Telnet, and occasionally took breaks to play Acrophobia on IRC. A class on numerical methods introduced me to FORTRAN and MATLAB, a drafting class covered AutoCAD, and in the architecture school I was introduced to rendering programs like Form-Z.
2001 Hearkening back to my early days playing text adventure games on my dad's Tandy, I got interested in interactive fiction. I decided to learn the TADS platform and coded several stories/games over the next couple of years, just for fun. Around this same time I started using the GIMP for graphic design.
2003 I worked with a volunteer at the church I was attending at the time to come up with the architecture for a new custom Access database to track donors and contributions. I also decided to learn CSS and, combining that with my knowledge of HTML, I was able to help maintain their website over the next decade or so.
2016 My sister opened her own salon, and I structured her website, managed her social media accounts, and did custom graphic design not just for those but for a host of marketing materials as well.
2019 Having recovered from cancer and chemo, I was finally in a position to think about a total career change. I started studying a few things on the side, like Python, but things really took off when I was accepted into LaunchCode's LC101 program and learned multiple languages, tools, and platforms in a short amount of time. I was voracious, using sites like HackerRank and CodeWars to solve hundreds of practice problems.
2020 Upon graduating from LC101 and completing a capstone project in the Liftoff program, I began an apprenticeship where I continued to learn new technologies. I also began working as a mentor for students in the CoderGirl program at LaunchCode. I have created over 50 supplemental practice exercises/projects for students and held several live tutorials to help them prepare for larger graded assignments.
2021 I completed the comprehensive course on Angular at Udemy and continue to add new material to my collection of practice exercises for students.
